Bad Debt Suspension / Patient is suspended from care for financial reasons

Instructions for Use: Call Center Agents should familiarize themselves with the following guidelines in the event they receive a call regarding a suspended account from a patient or clinical staff member.

Suspension Cycle:

Frequently Asked Questions/Statements:

Q: I need to schedule an appointment with Suspended Status.

Thank you for calling regarding your account. Your account is currently suspended for financial reasons. Is your appointment in reference to a recent hospital stay or work comp appointment? (If yes, see exception policy). To be reinstated you will need to contact the collections agency to set up payment arrangements on your account. Would you like me to transfer the call to the agency so they can assist you?

Q: I set up a payment plan, when will my account be reinstated?

Thank you for setting up the payment plan with agency. Once the agency notifies us of the first payment, we will update your account in 24-48 hours. You will receive a letter in the mail to notify you the suspension has been removed.

Account Statuses:

Status Meaning
Pending Bad Debt Dismissal Account is under review of suspension policy. Patient must follow payment plan guidelines to prevent suspension. Payment plans must be on auto-deduction.
Suspended Bad Debt Final Account has been Suspended for failure to set up payment arrangements on bad debt. Patient is required to set up payment plan with the agency on autopay in order to be reinstated. Once suspended any existing appointments will be cancelled from care.
CA Payment Plan Account was previously suspended but reinstated after meeting the requirements to list suspension. Failure to maintain on-time payments may result in Suspension Final.
SuspBDFinal Account is Suspended until all bad debt is paid in full.

Scheduling appointment requests on a suspended account

If patient calls to schedule their appointment, advise then that they will be able to be seen for 30 days from the time of the suspension letter, for medical emergencies only.
Note: If patient is on the phone stating it is an emergency, contact center (tier 2) can call the Collections Department to inform them.
If the office calls and needs to schedule an appointment, please consult with the appropriate contact center supervisor before turning them

Determine if payment plan is already set-up and first payment collected.

Yes

Advise caller that they must allow 24-48 hours for the suspension to be lifted.
Note:

  • If patient postdate payments, the Collections Department is not notified until the first payment is processed.
  • The Collections Department will not receive an email from the agency if payment plan doesn't fall within the guidelines, or if it does not satisfy the entire collection balance.
  • The Payment Plan amount in the letter may change if the collections balance increases or decreases thus patient should contact agency to go over the collection balance with them.
  • Inform the patient that any appointments that they may have had have been canceled and they should reach out to the office to reschedule their appointments.
